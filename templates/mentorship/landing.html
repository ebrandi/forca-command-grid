Why sign up as a Cadet

  • ✓Skip the expensive lessons. One hour with a veteran saves a month of dying to things you never saw coming.
  • ✓A clear path, not a firehose. Field Exercises take you from docking up to holding tackle on a real fleet, one step at a time.
  • ✓Someone in your corner. A name you can ping without feeling like you're bothering the whole corp.
  • ✓Earn as you learn. Completed exercises can pay ISK, points and recognition — see below.

Why mentor as a Veteran

  • ✓Build the fleet you want to fly with. Every Cadet you train is another competent pilot on comms next month.
  • ✓Recognition that sticks. Mentor badges, certifications and standing on the leadership board.
  • ✓Light touch, real impact. You pick your areas, your timezone and your pace — the Grid handles the matching and the paperwork.
  • ✓Give back what you were given. Every veteran here was once the confused newbro asking where the undock button is.

How it works

1

Register a role

Sign up as a Cadet, a Veteran Mentor, or both. Tell us your goals, areas and timezone.

2

Get matched

The Grid suggests best-fit pairings by area, timezone and goals. You approve — nobody is forced together.

3

Run Field Exercises

Work through a Track together — first fit, first fleet, first ratting site — and debrief each one.

4

Earn recognition

Completed exercises are validated, then Cadet and Mentor both collect their rewards.

What you earn

Progress is real recognition, not a participation ribbon. Depending on how leadership has tuned the programme, completing exercises and certifications can grant:

ISK rewards

Direct payouts for milestones — for the Cadet who did the work and the Mentor who taught it.

Contribution points

Points on the corp recognition ledger that count toward your standing.

Badges

Tiered mentor and cadet badges that show on your profile and dashboard.

Certifications

Proof you can fly the doctrine, hold tackle, or lead a small gang — recognised corp-wide.

Honest validation & privacy

  • Some steps auto-check. Where the Grid can confirm a fact from EVE data — a skill trained, a ship owned — it ticks the box for you.
  • The rest is a Mentor sign-off. Things a machine can't judge — "did the debrief, showed up, gets it" — your Mentor confirms by hand. No gaming it.
  • ESI is opt-in and minimal. We only ask for a scope when an exercise genuinely needs it, and never more than that. You can join and progress plenty without granting any.
Commitment: a few hours a week is plenty — set your own pace, pause any time.
