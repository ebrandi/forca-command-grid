Learn Planetary Industry

A five-minute primer. By the end you'll know what to build and why the planner asks what it asks.

Why bother?

Income while you sleep

PI is the closest thing EVE has to passive income. Once a colony is set up it keeps producing; you just reset extractors and haul. A handful of planets can quietly fund your PLEX, your ammo, or a corp's fuel supply. It scales from "one planet, ten minutes a week" to "a factory network feeding capital construction".

Two kinds of planet

Extraction vs factory

  • Extraction planet — an Extractor Control Unit pulls a raw P0 resource; Basic Industry Facilities refine it into P1. You export P1 (or feed it onward). Its output is set by how you place extractor heads and how often you reset.
  • Factory planet — no extraction. It imports cheaper materials and combines them into higher-tier goods (P2, P3, P4). Its output is set by how many factories you can fit and feed.

What decides your profit

Market price

Where and what you sell. The planner prices against a hub you choose.

Customs (POCO) tax

Charged every time you launch goods to/from a planet. High in hostile space.

Hauling

Getting output to market costs time or ISK — especially from null/wormhole.

Sales tax & broker

Market fees when you sell. Corp buyback trades a bit of margin for convenience.

Command Grid takes all of these, applies your assumptions, and shows the estimated net — with every assumption on the table so you can see exactly how the number was reached.
