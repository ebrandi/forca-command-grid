## Doctrines and readiness

The **doctrine library** (`/doctrines/`) is the corp's catalogue of standard fits, grouped
into named doctrines. Browsing it (and the companion **Shipyard**) may be restricted by
leadership to members or opened to allies or the public, depending on the corp's
preference.

Your own tools here are member-only:

- **My readiness** (`/doctrines/my-readiness/`) — every doctrine you can fly *right now*,
  ranked by how close you are to the ones you can't, with the exact skills or modules still
  missing.
- **Doctrine detail and readiness** (`/doctrines/<id>/`, `/doctrines/<id>/readiness/`) —
  drill into a specific doctrine and see a per-fit skill verdict.
- **Pre-fleet prep** (`/doctrines/<id>/prep/`) — a checklist to run through before an
  operation using that doctrine.

If you're asked to fly a doctrine you're not quite ready for, this is the first place to
check what's missing.

## Skill plans

**Skill plans** (`/skills/`) turn a doctrine gap into an ordered, ETA-estimated training
queue: what to train, in what order, and roughly when you'll be done. If your training
queue is about to run dry, you can opt into a nudge that reminds you before it does. Skill
plans are personal — yours belong to you.

## Fleet operations

**Operations** (`/operations/`) is where leadership schedules deployments, home defence,
structure timers, doctrine rollouts, and combat or mining fleets, each with a target date,
doctrines, and a ship composition.

As a member you can:

- Browse upcoming and past ops.
- **RSVP** to signal you intend to show up.
- **Commit to a specific ship slot** — this is a race-safe claim, so you won't end up
  double-booked into the same slot as someone else.
- **Self-mark your attendance**, which — if the op has fleet tracking enabled — is
  automatically corroborated against the in-game fleet roster for accurate participation
  (PAP) credit.

Committing to a slot is the best way to help an FC know the fleet composition is covered
before undock.

## Ship replacement (SRP)

**SRP** (`/srp/`) is the corp's ship-replacement programme. It is planning and
record-keeping only — the app itself never moves ISK; leadership handles the actual
payout in-game. If you lose a ship in an op that's eligible for SRP:

1. **File a claim** (`/srp/claim/`) referencing your loss.
2. An SRP manager reviews it — approves, denies, or adjusts the payout — against the
   corp's current SRP program rules.
3. Track your claim's status from your own SRP page (`/srp/`); once approved and paid,
   it's marked as such.

Whoever manages the SRP queue can never approve or pay out their own claim — that
separation is enforced by the app, not just policy.
